Although the address of each camera is provided, the information on corresponding latitude and longtitude is missing. The Google Map Geocoding API was applied to collect such information for better geographic mapping visulization.

Exploratory Analysis --- Identify Strong Attributes

Temperature

Every 1F temperature increase brings up the total number of red light and speeding violations by 8 and 7, respectively. Putting these numbers into context: if we approximate the temperature change from day to day is around 10 - 20 F. The total number for red light and speeding violations could change by 100 - 200. It is roughly 2% - 3% change, comparing with the average number of daily violations.

Precipitation

The precipitation reported here is defined as the sum of rain and melted snow/ice. Every 1 inch precipitation increase brings down the total number of red light and speeding violations by 70 and 750, respectively. The speeding violation dependence on precipitation is about 5 - 6 times stronger than the one for the red light violations. If a heavy rain brings 1 inch of precipitation, it decreases the speeding and red light violations by 25% and 5%, compared with days with no precipitation.

Weekday

Fun facts: Red light data: people more careless on Saturdays, and more carefull on Mondays. Speeding data: people with bad adventure habit are more excited on Fridays and less excited on Sundays. The weekday dependence is stronger for red light violations (Sat violations are 27% more than Mon violations), and weaker for speeding violations (Fri violations are 15% more than Sun violations).

Location

The location of cameras appears to be a strong feature determining the number of violations.
